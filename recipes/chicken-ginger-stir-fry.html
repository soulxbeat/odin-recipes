Chicken Ginger Stir-fry

Description:

Deceptively easy to make, yet so full of flavor. This dish is chock-full of tasty veggies and tender, juicy chicken breast strips.

Starting with sesame oil, ginger, and garlic, the base flavor is the foundation for the sweet mirin and savory soy sauce to build upon. Quick sauteing the veggies keeps them cripsy on the outside and tender on the inside. The chicken can be cut into strips or cubed chunks, whichever you prefer!

Ingredients:

  • 4 cloves of garlic, minced or crushed (or more if you're a garlic fiend like me!)
  • 1 tablespoon of fresh grated ginger root
  • 1 pound (lb) of chicken breast, cut into strips or cubed
  • 2 tablespoons of sesame oil, divded
  • 1 red bell pepper, cut into strips
  • 1 handful of shiitake mushrooms, rinsed and patted dry
  • 1 head of broccoli, cut into florets
  • 2 large carrots, julienned or cut into rounds
  • 1 medium sized yellow onion, diced
  • 1 1/2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 2 teaspoons mirin

Steps:

  1. Heat a tablespoon of sesame oil over medium heat in a skillet or wok
  2. When hot, add garlic cloves, ginger, and onion. Stir frequently for 2-3 minutes, until fragrant
  3. Add chicken and cook unitl mostly cooked through, about 4-5 minutes and remove from wok or skillet
  4. Add the second tablespoon of oil and shiitake mushrooms. Sautee for 4 to 5 minutes until golden brown
  5. Turn the heat up to medium high and add the rest of the veggies as well as the soy sauce and mirin, stirring frequently for 4 to 5 minutes
  6. Add chicken back and cook for another 2 minutes until chicken is cooked through
  7. Remove from heat and serve with your favorite noodles or rice - or eat as is for a no-carb alternative!
